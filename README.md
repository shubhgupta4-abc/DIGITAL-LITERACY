#  Introduction

This repository contains my **Digital Literacy Portfolio**, created as part of the **CSE0001 – Digital Literacy course** at VIT Bhopal University.

For this project, we were asked to imagine ourselves as a **Student Digital Ambassador**. The idea behind this role is to help fellow students understand how to use digital tools responsibly, communicate professionally online, and stay safe while using the internet.

The project is divided into **five different tasks**, and each task focuses on an important part of digital literacy. Through these activities, I explored topics such as building an online portfolio, practicing coding, understanding email etiquette, and learning about cybercrime awareness.

All the work related to the tasks is organized into different folders inside this repository.

In the first task, I created a **digital literacy awareness infographic** to explain the importance of being digitally responsible and informed.

#  Task 4 – Professional Email & Online Etiquette

This task focused on improving **professional communication skills**, especially when sending emails.

I wrote two sample emails:

**Email 1 – Assignment Extension Request**
A professional email written by a student requesting an extension for an assignment deadline.

**Email 2 – Internship Inquiry**
An email expressing interest in a potential summer internship opportunity at a company.

Both emails follow standard professional email structure, including:

* A clear subject line
* Proper greeting
* Structured message body
* Polite closing and signature

In addition to the emails, I also created a **Social Media Do’s and Don’ts checklist** to encourage responsible digital behavior among college students.

 All related files are included in the **task-4-email-etiquette** folder.

#  Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness

The final task focuses on **cybercrime awareness and prevention**.

I researched a cybercrime topic such as **phishing, UPI fraud, identity theft, cyberbullying, or ransomware** and created a case study explaining how the attack works and who it typically targets.

The goal of this task was to help students understand the risks of online activities and learn how to protect themselves.

I also created a **“Stay Safe Online” checklist** with practical safety tips, including:

* Never sharing OTPs or passwords
* Verifying payment links before making transactions
* Using strong and unique passwords
* Enabling two-factor authentication
* Avoiding suspicious messages or emails
